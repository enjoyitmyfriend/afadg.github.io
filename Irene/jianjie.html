Harry Potter

Harry Potter It is a series of seven fantasy novels written by British author J. K. Rowling from 1997 to 2007. The first six of them take Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizarding as the main stage, describing the protagonist - young wizard student Harry Potter before and after six years of learning life and adventure stories; The seventh book follows Harry Potter as he searches for Horcruxes and destroys Lord Voldemort during the Second Wizarding War.

Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizarding, based on the fantasy novel Harry Potter by J.K. Rowling, is located in the Gothic style of Hogwarts Castle in Scotland, England, its exact location is uncertain, as it cannot be plotted. Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizarding was founded around 990 by four of the greatest wizards of the time: Godric Gryffindor, Salazar Slytherin, Roina Ravenclaw, and Helga Hufflepuff.

Gryffindor for fire, Ravenclaw for wind, Hufflepuff for earth, Slatling for water, when the four elements exist at the same time, constitute a complete system. But they are also known to be independent. ----J.K. Rowling

The story begins when Harry Potter is sent as an orphan to live with his aunt, where he suffers neglect and abuse for ten years. However, when Harry was eleven years old, his life changed dramatically. Harry learns that he is a wizard and is invited to study magic at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. At Hogwarts, Harry meets two of his best friends, Hermione Granger and RON Weasley, with whom he experiences many adventures and challenges. Harry also discovers that he has a mysterious lightning-shaped scar left on his body by the evil dark wizard Lord Voldemort when he was a baby. Voldemort attempts to kill Harry, but his attempts fail, causing his own powers to weaken. As Harry progresses through Hogwarts, he begins to realize that he has a close bond with Lord Voldemort and becomes a key figure in the fight against him. Throughout the series, Harry and his friends continue to uncover Voldemort's secrets in preparation for the final confrontation. Themes of growth, courage, friendship, and sacrifice run through the series, revealing a world full of magic and wonder. The Harry Potter series is not only loved by readers, but has also been adapted into a series of successful films and become a global cultural phenomenon.
