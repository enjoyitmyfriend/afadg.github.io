HOGWARTS

Historical Background: Hogwarts was founded around 990 AD by four founders: Godric Gryffindor, Helga Hufflepuff, Roina Ravenclaw, and Salazar Slytherin. The school adopts a seven-year school system and is one of the three major magical schools in Europe, known for its unique educational approach and rich magic curriculum.
Geographical location: The school is located in the Scottish Highlands, surrounded by mountains, lakes in the south, and forbidden forests spreading in the west of the castle. The overall architectural style is Gothic, and the castle is eight stories high, full of mystery and ancient atmosphere.
The Four Houses: Hogwarts has four houses, Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw and Slytherin, each with its own unique representative colours and animals. New students are assigned to one of the colleges through a sorting cap upon admission, a process that takes into account not only the personality of the student, but also the unique spirit of the college. Gryffindor was known for its courage and chivalry; Hufflepuff is known for the integrity, loyalty and sense of impartiality of its students; Ravenclaw students are marked by intelligence and erudite; Slytherin was known for his ambition and shrewdness.
Academic courses: Hogwarts offers a variety of magic-related courses, including compulsory and elective courses such as History of magic, potions, and divination. These courses are designed to develop students' magical skills and theoretical knowledge to give them a solid foundation for their future careers.
Campus life: Campus life is colorful, in addition to daily study, there are a variety of club activities, Quidditch and house Cup competitions. Every year on Halloween and Christmas, Hogwarts will hold a celebration banquet to promote communication and unity among students.
Faculty: Hogwarts has a team of experienced and knowledgeable faculty members who are not only deeply knowledgeable in their respective fields of expertise, but also committed to cultivating students' moral and magical skills.
Culture and tradition: Hogwarts emphasizes the motto "Never tease a sleeping dragon" as a symbol of caution and respect. Various school traditions and rituals, such as sorting ceremonies, Halloween banquets, and year-end dinners, are an important part of campus culture and enhance students' sense of belonging and honor.
Famous alumni: Hogwarts has produced many famous wizards and witches, including Harry Potter, Albus Dumbledore and Hermione Granger, who have achieved notable achievements in the wizarding and Muggle worlds and have earned the school a good reputation.
